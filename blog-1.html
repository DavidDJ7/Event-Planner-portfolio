How to plan a traditional Tamil wedding in 6 months

May 20, 2026 8 min read By Karthikeyan S.

Planning a Tamil wedding can feel like orchestrating a symphony — there are rituals to coordinate, vendors to vet, extended family to satisfy, and a thousand small decisions that add up. But with the right timeline, it becomes manageable. We've planned 850+ events, and this six-month guide is the same framework we use with every client.

Pro tip: Tamil wedding peak season runs from November to February. If you're targeting these months, start planning 8-10 months out. Our NRI clients often start a full year ahead.

Month 6: The foundation

This is the most important month. Everything flows from decisions made here.

  • Fix the Muhurtham date. Consult your family priest or an astrologer to identify auspicious dates. Have 3-4 backup dates — prime slots at venues and vendors book quickly.
  • Set a budget. Have the honest conversation with both families. A typical Tamil wedding in a metro city ranges from ₹8L to ₹25L for 300-800 guests. Break it down by category: venue (25-30%), catering (20-25%), decor (15-20%), photography (8-10%), attire (6-8%), entertainment (5-8%), miscellaneous (5-10%).
  • Choose your event model. Are you going full-service with us, semi-managed, or completely DIY? This decision affects every subsequent step.
  • Create a guest list. Start with a rough estimate. The venue and catering budget depend on this number.

Month 5: Venue & major vendors

With the date locked and budget set, it's time to secure the big pieces.

  • Book the venue. For traditional weddings, consider Kalyana Mandapams (budget-friendly, familiar with rituals), hotel ballrooms (premium, AC comfort), temple premises (spiritual, limited capacity), or destination resorts (for intimate affairs). Visit at least 3 venues before deciding.
  • Lock your photographer. The best wedding photographers in Tamil Nadu book 4-6 months in advance. Look for someone who speaks Tamil and understands the flow of Iyer/Iyengar/Chettiar ceremonies — timing is everything.
  • Engage a decorator. Your wedding's visual identity comes from the decor. Share reference images, discuss the mandapam design, and confirm flower types (jasmine, marigold, rose are the staples).
  • Finalize catering. Schedule a tasting session. Discuss menu options — traditional sadya, live counters, fusion items. Confirm per-plate cost and minimum guarantee.

Month 4: Design & creative

This is where the wedding starts to take visual shape.

  • Design the mandapam. The mandapam is the centerpiece of a Tamil wedding. Work with your decorator on the concept — traditional floral pillars, contemporary minimal, or fusion.
  • Plan the bridal entry. Whether it's a classic pallakku entry, a grand reveal, or a walking entry through flower petals — this moment needs choreography.
  • Choose wedding colors. Gold and red are classic. Modern couples are experimenting with blush + champagne, mint + coral, or deep jewel tones. Coordinate across decor, bridal wear, and bridesmaid outfits.
  • Book entertainment. Live music for the sangeet, DJ for the reception, traditional dancers for the wedding — book early to get the best talent.

Month 3: Logistics & guest experience

The guest experience separates a good wedding from an unforgettable one.

  • Send invitations. Go digital with a wedding website for RSVPs, but keep printed cards for elders — tradition matters. Send save-the-dates to out-of-town guests.
  • Arrange guest transportation and accommodation. If many guests are traveling, block hotel rooms at negotiated rates and arrange pickup/drop from railway stations and airports.
  • Hire an emcee. A bilingual emcee who can switch between Tamil and English effortlessly keeps both generations engaged. They should understand the ritual flow to make timely announcements.
  • Plan the wedding wardrobe. Finalize bridal silk saree, groom's veshti and angavastram, bridesmaid outfits, and parents' attire. Allow time for tailoring and alterations.

Month 2: Rehearsals & details

Everything gets tighter now. This is where planners earn their keep.

  • Rehearse. Walk through the ceremony with the priest. Understand each ritual — Kashi Yatra, Oonjal, Saptapadi, Kanyadaanam. Ensure the bride and groom know what to expect.
  • Confirm all vendor bookings. Call every vendor—caterer, decorator, photographer, makeup artist, musician — and reconfirm dates, times, and deliverables.
  • Finalize the menu. Confirm the final guest count and finalize the menu with the caterer. Consider dietary restrictions (Jain, keto, vegan options).
  • Create a day-of timeline. Map every minute from Muhurtham to reception. Share it with the wedding party, vendors, and venue manager.

Month 1: The final stretch

This is execution mode. Trust your team and stay present.

  • Packing & logistics. Ensure all wedding items are packed and transported — silk sarees, jewelry, pooja items, gifts, return gifts.
  • Beauty & grooming. Finalize hair and makeup trials. Schedule mehendi artist. Keep skincare simple — hydration and sleep.
  • Rest. The week before the wedding, delegate everything to your planner or a family point person. Sleep, hydrate, and prepare to enjoy your own wedding.
Remember: The goal is not perfection — it's presence. We've seen parents stress over centerpiece height while missing their child's smile. Delegate the details. Show up for the moments. That's what stays with you.

Whether you plan every detail yourself or hand it to us completely, this timeline gives you a roadmap. Need a partner for the journey? We're here.
