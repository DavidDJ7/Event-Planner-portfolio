## 1. Business Foundation Questions

### What is the business name?
**தமிழ் நிகழ்வுகள் — Thamizh Nikazhvigal (Tamil Events)**

### Do you have a tagline or slogan?
**"ஒவ்வொரு நிகழ்வும் ஒரு காவியம்" — "Every event is an epic."**

### What is the vision of the company?
To become Tamil Nadu's most trusted and emotionally connected event management brand — where every celebration reflects the richness of Tamil culture blended with world-class execution.

### What inspired the business to start?
The founder grew up in a small town in Tamil Nadu, watching families struggle to coordinate weddings and festivals. Relatives would travel across villages, caterers would cancel last minute, and the beauty of the celebration was often lost in the stress. The desire to bring joy back into celebrations — to let families actually *enjoy* their events instead of managing them — was the spark. The love for Tamil traditions, temple ceremonies, and the desire to present them with modern sophistication drove the founding.

### How did you start?
It began in 2014 with a single wedding for the founder's cousin in Trichy. With just ₹50,000 savings, a rented Tata Ace for decoration transport, and a team of five college friends, they managed a 1,200-guest wedding. The client's family was overwhelmed with joy. Word spread. By 2016, they had handled 15 weddings. By 2018, they incorporated as a private limited company. Today, they have offices in Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, and Trichy.

### What makes your event management company unique?
- **Deep Tamil cultural expertise** — We don't just *do* Tamil weddings; we *understand* them. The difference between a Chettinad wedding and a Kongu wedding, the correct placement of the *Pandal*, the right *Muhurtham* timing protocols.
- **End-to-end stress guarantee** — We assign a dedicated "Event Parent" (நிகழ்வுப் பெற்றோர்) who handles *everything* so the family can be guests at their own event.
- **No hidden costs** — Transparent pricing with itemized billing is our core promise.
- **Technology + Tradition** — We use proprietary software for vendor coordination, budget tracking, and guest management while keeping every ceremony authentic.

### Who are your main competitors?
- WeddingWish (Chennai-based)
- Dream Makers Events (Coimbatore)
- Grand Telugu Events (extending into TN)
- Kalai Niram Events (Madurai)
- Local independent wedding planners in each district
- High-end hotels' in-house wedding teams (ITC Grand Chola, Leela Palace, Vivanta)

### How do you differ from your competitors?
| Aspect | Competitors | Thamizh Nikazhvigal |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Cultural depth | Surface-level | Ceremony-by-ceremony expertise |
| Pricing transparency | Hidden costs | 100% itemized quotes |
| Team dedication | Rotating coordinators | One "Event Parent" per client |
| Regional coverage | Single city | 4 offices across TN |
| Post-event support | None | 30-day follow-up and family album delivery |

### Is your brand positioned as premium, luxury, or budget-friendly?
**Premium with heart.** We are not the cheapest (that's not our goal), but we are not inaccessible. We position ourselves as *aspirational but achievable* — a family saving for a year can afford us, and a celebrity can trust us. Our average wedding package ranges from ₹8–25 lakhs.

### What brand personality should the website reflect? (Luxury / Minimal / Modern / Traditional / Corporate / Youthful)
A **harmonious blend of Traditional + Modern + Premium**.
- **Traditional** because we celebrate Tamil culture
- **Modern** because our execution is tech-enabled and sleek
- **Premium** because we don't compromise on quality

The site should feel like walking into a beautifully decorated Tamil wedding hall — rich with culture, but impeccably organized.

## 3. Target Audience Questions

### Who is the target audience?
- **Primary:** Tamil families in Tamil Nadu planning weddings (ages 25–55, decision-makers are parents aged 45–55 and the couple aged 25–32)
- **Secondary:**
  - Corporate event managers (conferences, product launches, team outings)
  - College cultural fest organizers
  - NRIs (Non-Resident Indians) of Tamil origin planning events in TN
  - High-net-worth individuals planning engagement, reception, baby shower

### What is the age group of your customers?
25–55 years old. The bride/groom are 25–32, parents are 45–55, and both are involved in decision-making.

### Which locations or regions are you targeting?
Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Trichy, Salem, Tirunelveli, Puducherry, Vellore, Thanjavur, Erode, and eventually the Tamil diaspora globally.

### Are you targeting high-budget clients?
Yes, but not exclusively. We cater to:
- **Premium segment (₹15–40 lakhs):** 30% of our clients
- **Mid-premium segment (₹8–15 lakhs):** 50% of our clients
- **Select corporate and NRI events (₹20 lakhs+):** 20%

### Are your clients mostly corporate customers?
**80% are wedding/event customers**, 20% are corporate. However, corporate revenue per event is higher, so revenue split is roughly 60% weddings / 40% corporate.

### Are you focusing on wedding/event customers?
Yes, weddings are our heartbeat. But we also actively serve engagements (நிச்சயதார்த்தம்), receptions, baby showers (வளைகாப்பு / சீமந்தம்), and religious ceremonies like *Gruhapravesam* and *Ayush Homam*.

### What devices do your users mainly use? (Mobile / Desktop)
**70% Mobile, 30% Desktop.** Most initial browsing happens on mobile (often via Instagram or WhatsApp referral), but final pricing and package comparison happens on desktop. Mobile-first design is non-negotiable.

### What are the biggest pain points of your audience?
1. **"We don't know what we don't know."** First-time wedding planners are overwhelmed.
2. **Hidden costs.** Families fear being cheated — a very common experience in TN.
3. **Coordination fatigue.** Managing caterer, decorator, photographer, priest, venue, and guest list is exhausting.
4. **Last-minute vendor cancellations.** Especially in peak seasons (June–Aug, Nov–Feb).
5. **Family disagreements.** Multiple family members have conflicting visions.
6. **Language barriers.** Many older family members are more comfortable in Tamil, but most event websites are English-only.
7. **Dietary and ritual customization.** Every family has unique traditions and food preferences (pure vegetarian, specific community dishes).
8. **Budget anxiety.** Fear of going over budget due to unexpected expenses.
9. **Guest list management.** Tracking RSVPs, dietary restrictions, transport, and accommodation for 200–2,000 guests.
10. **Post-event blues.** No structured follow-up or photo/video delivery timeline.

## 5. Customer Journey Questions

### What should customers feel when they first open the website?
**"Ah, I'm in safe hands. They understand my culture and my dream."** A sense of warmth, trust, and awe. They should see themselves in the imagery — real Tamil families, real Tamil celebrations, not stock photography.

### What should users immediately understand about the business?
1. **We are Tamil Nadu's premium event planners.**
2. **We handle everything — you just enjoy.**
3. **Our work is beautiful and culturally authentic.**
4. **You can trust us — transparent pricing, real reviews, verified team.**
5. **We speak your language (Tamil + English).**

### What should be the first action users take on the website?
**See our work.** The gallery/portfolio should be immediately prominent. After seeing 3–5 events, they should feel compelled to click **"Plan Your Event"** or **"Get Free Quote"**.

### How simple should the inquiry process be?
**As simple as sending a WhatsApp message.**
- Step 1: Select event type (dropdown)
- Step 2: Enter name + phone number
- Step 3: Click "Get callback in 5 minutes" or "Chat on WhatsApp"
That's it. No long forms. No mandatory email.

### How should the booking process work?
1. **Inquiry** → Website/WhatsApp form (1 minute)
2. **Consultation call** → Within 4 hours (team calls)
3. **In-person meeting** → At our office or their home (within 48 hours)
4. **Proposal & quote** → Itemized PDF within 24 hours of meeting
5. **Agreement & advance payment** → 25% booking advance
6. **Planning phase** → Dedicated Event Parent assigned, vendor coordination starts
7. **Event execution** → On-ground team, real-time updates to family
8. **Post-event** → Thank you note, photo delivery timeline, feedback request

### Should users be able to contact directly through WhatsApp?
**Absolutely essential.** WhatsApp is how Tamil Nadu communicates. Every page must have a sticky WhatsApp button that opens `wa.me/91XXXXXXXXXX` with a pre-filled message: *"Hi! I'm interested in planning my [event type]. Please share details."*

### Should direct call booking be available?
**Yes.** A prominent "Call Now" button with the phone number. We found that many older decision-makers (parents of the bride/groom) prefer to call directly.

### Should there be a quotation request form?
**Yes, but keep it minimal.** Only 5 fields:
1. Event type
2. Expected date
3. Approximate guest count
4. Name
5. Phone number
Long forms kill conversions.

### Should consultation booking be available?
**Yes.** Users should be able to book a free 30-minute consultation:
- Online (Google Meet / Zoom)
- At our office (Chennai / Coimbatore / Madurai / Trichy)
- At their home (for high-value packages ₹20L+)

| Charcoal Black | `#2C2C2C` | Text color |

### Do you prefer a dark or light theme?
**Light theme with rich, warm tones.** A dark theme might feel too modern/corporate. We want warmth, tradition, and elegance. However, a **dark mode toggle** as an option would be a nice premium touch.

### Should the design feel luxurious and elegant?
**Yes, but approachable.** The luxury should come through quality imagery, whitespace, smooth animations, and attention to detail — not through flashy elements or gold overload.

### Should the website have a minimal aesthetic look?
**Clean minimalism with cultural accents.** Minimal layout, but the culture comes through via:
- Tamil typography headlines
- Traditional motif patterns (kolam borders, temple carvings as design elements)
- Warm texture backgrounds (subtle silk patterns)
- Real event imagery (not stock)

### What type of typography/font style do you prefer?
- **Headlines:** A serif font like *Playfair Display* or *Cormorant Garamond* for elegance, paired with a Tamil serif font (like *Noto Sans Tamil* or *Tamil MN*)
- **Body:** A clean sans-serif like *Inter* or *Montserrat*
- **Tamil text:** *Noto Sans Tamil* (variable weight) for maximum readability
- Headlines should have a traditional calligraphy feel, body should be ultra-readable.

### What level of animations should be used? (Minimal / Smooth premium / Cinematic)
**Smooth premium animations.**
- Subtle parallax scrolling
- Fade-in and slide-up on section entrance
- Smooth image gallery transitions (masonry with hover zoom)
- Video backgrounds that load lazily
- NO flashing, bouncing, or distracting animations
- Everything should feel like a premium magazine, not a carnival website

### Do you already have real event photos/videos?
**Yes.** We have a library of 10,000+ high-resolution photos and 200+ event videos spanning 8 years. All real clients, all real Tamil events. We will NOT use stock photography.

## 21. Our Origin Story

### Why did you start this business?
It started with a simple observation at a family wedding in 2013. The bride's mother — a sweet, gentle woman — was running around from 4 AM, checking the food, managing the decorators, arguing with the caterer, directing the photographer. By the time the actual wedding happened at 9 AM, she was exhausted. She didn't get to see her daughter's face when she first saw the groom. She missed the moment.

The founder thought: *This is wrong. The mother of the bride should be sitting in the front row, crying happy tears, not sweating under the kitchen tent.*

That wedding — that exhausted mother — was the reason. Every event since has been about giving families back their moments.

### How did you start?
**Timeline:**

- **2013:** Founder helped plan a cousin's wedding in Trichy. No budget, no team, just passion. It went beautifully. Word spread.
- **2014:** Officially launched as "Tamil Events" with ₹50,000 capital. First office was a 10x10 room in Trichy. First team: 5 college friends.
- **2015:** 12 weddings completed. Hired first full-time employee. Moved to a bigger space.
- **2016:** 25 weddings. Started corporate events. Bought first inventory (sound system, basic lighting).
- **2017:** Incorporated as Thamizh Nikazhvigal Pvt. Ltd. Office in Chennai opens.
- **2018:** 60 weddings. Team grows to 15. Start getting NRI inquiries.
- **2019:** Launch Coimbatore office. First ₹1 crore revenue year.
- **2020:** COVID hit. Pivoted to small intimate weddings. Survived on 15 micro-weddings. Built online presence.
- **2021:** Post-COVID boom. 100+ events. Madurai office opens.
- **2022:** 200+ events. Team of 40. Invested in in-house photography and video.
- **2023:** Won "Best Wedding Planner — Tamil Nadu" award. Trichy office expands.
- **2024:** 400+ events. 60 team members. Launched NRI wedding division.
- **2025:** 600+ events. 4 offices. Revenue crosses ₹5 crores.
- **2026 (now):** 850+ events. Planning tech upgrade, franchise model, and Tamil diaspora expansion.

### What was the biggest challenge in the beginning?
**Credibility.** When you start as a 23-year-old with no office, no portfolio, and a dream — no one trusts you with their wedding. The first 5 clients were family friends who took a chance. The first "stranger" client came in 2015 — a family who saw our work at a relative's wedding. That gave us our first real testimonial and our first Google review. From there, it snowballed.

### What kept you going during tough times?
The emotional payoff. Every time a bride's mother hugged us and cried, saying *"We actually enjoyed our daughter's wedding"* — that was fuel for the next 6 months. The 2020 COVID lockdown was the hardest. We had 25 weddings canceled in 2 months. But the families who trusted us with small 50-guest weddings during that time reminded us why we exist — to make every moment beautiful, no matter the size.

Things will go wrong. The caterer's van will break down. It will rain on your outdoor event. The priest will be late. The bride's dress will have a loose thread. But when you have a team that handles each crisis without the client ever knowing — that's the art of event management.

### What is one thing you wish every client knew before planning an event?
**"Your guests will not notice 90% of the things you're stressed about."**

That centerpiece you spent 3 hours choosing? Guests will glance at it for 2 seconds. That font on the invitation? Nobody analyzes it. What they *will* remember: the food, the warmth, the music, whether they felt welcomed, and whether the couple looked happy. Spend your energy on those things.

### How do you handle difficult clients?
With **empathy and boundaries**.

- **Listen first.** Most "difficult" clients are just anxious clients who haven't been heard.
- **Educate, don't dismiss.** "I understand you want this. Here's why it may not work, and here are 3 alternatives."
- **Document everything.** Written agreements prevent "but you said" situations.
- **Know when to walk away.** If values don't align (e.g., client wants unethical practices), we politely decline. Reputation is everything.

### What is your pricing philosophy?
**Value-based, not cost-plus.**

We don't just charge for flowers, food, and labor. We charge for:
- Peace of mind (you won't get calls at 2 AM)
- Time saved (we do in 2 weeks what takes a family 6 months)
- Expertise (knowing the difference between a Chola and Pandya wedding setup)
- Relationships (our vendors give us priority because of our reputation)
- Crisis management (when something goes wrong, we fix it quietly)

A family isn't paying for services. They're paying for the *guarantee* that they will enjoy their own event.

### How do you select and manage your vendors?
**Rigorous vetting, long-term relationships.**

- Every vendor is personally met, their work reviewed, and references checked.
- We negotiate bulk rates but ensure vendors are paid fairly and on time.
- We have a "Vendor Code of Conduct" — punctuality, hygiene, professional behavior, no cancellation without 30-day notice.
- We rotate vendors based on client feedback and quality scores.
- Payment to vendors is always on time (often early), which ensures they prioritize our events.

### How do you handle last-minute emergencies?
We have a **"Red Book" protocol:**
1. Every event has a risk assessment document prepared 7 days before
2. Backup vendors identified for every category (caterer, decor, sound, photography)
3. Emergency fund (₹50,000 cash) always available on-site
4. On-ground team has direct contact with hospital, police, fire department
5. Founder's personal number is shared with every client — no layers of hierarchy in a crisis
6. We have never canceled an event. Not once in 10 years.

### What trends do you see in Tamil event planning?
1. **Intimate weddings are here to stay.** Post-COVID, families prefer 200–500 guests over 1000+.
2. **Day weddings are back.** Morning *Muhurtham* weddings with breakfast/lunch menus are trending over late-night events.
3. **Eco-consciousness.** Families asking for plantable return gifts, minimal firecrackers, bamboo decor.
4. **Fusion ceremonies.** Tamil + Western elements — *Mehendi* and *Sangeet* added to traditional Tamil weddings.
5. **Digital integration.** Wedding websites, QR code invites, live streaming for relatives abroad.
6. **Food diversity.** Even traditional families want food counters — Chinese, Italian, chat stations alongside the *sadya*.
7. **Brides leading planning.** More brides are educated, working professionals making design decisions (even paying for events themselves).

### What advice would you give to someone starting an event company?
1. **Don't start with grand visions.** Start with one small event. Do it so well that the next one finds you.
2. **Under-promise, over-deliver.** In this industry, word-of-mouth is oxygen.
3. **Invest in your team.** A solo event planner is a freelancer. A team is a company.
4. **Have a niche.** Don't try to do everything. Become the best at *something* (for us, it was Tamil weddings).
5. **Get everything in writing.** Contracts save friendships.
6. **Love the grind.** Events mean 18-hour days, weekends, holidays. You must love it.
7. **Save for slow seasons.** Event income is feast or famine. Plan financially.
